Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a rapidly growing cybercrime in India, targeting users of digital payment apps. 
It typically begins when a fraudster contacts the victim via phone, SMS, or messaging apps, posing as a bank official, customer care agent, 
or even a buyer/seller in an online marketplace.

Step-by-step, the scam unfolds as follows: 
First, the attacker gains the victim’s trust by offering help (e.g., resolving a payment issue or completing a transaction). 
Next, they send a “collect request” or a malicious payment link and instruct the victim to “enter the PIN to receive money.” 
Many users mistakenly believe entering their UPI PIN will credit money, but it actually authorizes a debit. 
Once the PIN is entered, the money is instantly transferred to the fraudster’s account. 
In some cases, screen-sharing apps are used to steal sensitive details.

The primary targets are new digital users, elderly individuals, and small business owners unfamiliar with UPI processes. 
Lack of awareness makes them especially vulnerable.

The consequences can be severe. 
Victims often lose significant sums of money, 
and recovery is difficult due to the instant nature of transactions. 
Emotional distress, loss of trust in digital systems, and financial instability are common outcomes. 
In real cases reported across India, victims have lost savings within minutes.

This case highlights the importance of awareness: 
never share UPI PINs, avoid unknown payment requests, and verify sources before making transactions.
