## Stay Safe Online – Checklist for College Students (India)
Use strong, unique passwords
Create different passwords for each account (email, social media, banking) and include a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.
Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)
Turn on 2FA for apps like Gmail, Instagram, and banking apps to add an extra layer of security.
Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks
Do not access banking, UPI apps, or personal accounts on public Wi-Fi in cafés or campuses.
Think before clicking links
Do not click on suspicious links in emails, SMS, or WhatsApp messages, especially those offering prizes, jobs, or urgent requests.
Never share OTPs or passwords
No legitimate company or bank will ask for your OTP, PIN, or password—never share these with anyone.
UPI Safety Tip: Don’t approve unknown collect requests
If you receive a “request to pay” on apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm, decline it unless you trust the sender.
UPI Safety Tip: Remember—PIN is only for sending money
You do NOT need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. Entering it always means you are authorizing a payment.
Download apps only from official stores
Use Google Play Store or Apple App Store and avoid third-party APKs that may contain malware.
Limit personal information on social media
Avoid sharing phone numbers, addresses, or travel plans publicly to reduce risk of identity theft or stalking.
Log out from shared devices
Always log out of accounts when using college computers or someone else’s device.
